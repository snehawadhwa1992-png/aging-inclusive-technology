AgeTech Design Framework

Five Evidence-Based Big Ideas with Design Guidelines for Creating Technology for Older Adults

1
Learning Infrastructure, Not Just Product Features
Design Guidelines
  • Identity-Affirming Design
  • Learning Through Low-Stakes Practice and Gamification
  • Design Cognitive-Friendly Interaction Layers
  • Create Coherent Ecosystems & Plan for Frictionless Entry and Exit
  • Trust-Building Through Transparency and Ongoing Support
2
Agency and Autonomy, Not Monitoring and Control
Design Guidelines
  • Design for Self-Directed, Empowering Experiences
  • Position Seniors as Primary Users, Not Caregivers
  • Connect to Purpose, Identity, and Contribution
  • Co-Design WITH Senior Adults, Not FOR Them
3
Holistic Wellbeing, Not Just Physical Health
Design Guidelines
  • Design for All Four Domains of Wellbeing
  • Design for Socially-Connected Aging
  • Include Cultural, Economic, and Contextual Accessibility
4
Meaningful Engagement, Not Just Time-Filling
Design Guidelines
  • Design for Intrinsic Motivation and User-Controlled Engagement
5
Adaptation, Personalization, and Accessible Interfaces
Design Guidelines
  • Design for Adaptation, Not Perfection
  • Address Specific Usability Barriers Through Multiple Modalities
  • Build Person-Centered Profiles
